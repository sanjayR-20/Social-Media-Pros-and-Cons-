1) Introduction

Overview

Social media platforms enable people to create, share, and discover content in real time. They influence communication, business, education, and culture by connecting users across the world at low cost and high speed.

  • Examples: Instagram, X (Twitter), Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube.
  • Core actions: posting, commenting, messaging, live streaming, communities.
  • Key trends: short‑form video, creator economy, social commerce, AI moderation.

2) Social Media Skills

Skills
  • Content Creation: Copywriting, basic design, video editing.
  • Platform Strategy: Understanding algorithms, timing, hashtags.
  • Community Management: Engaging replies, moderation, feedback.
  • Analytics: Reading insights (reach, CTR, watch time) to iterate.
  • Brand & Ethics: Tone, attribution, privacy, and authentic voice.

3) Advantages

Pros
  • Instant communication and global networking.
  • Learning resources and knowledge sharing.
  • Marketing reach for careers, startups, and creators.
  • Community support around shared interests.
  • Real‑time news and event discovery.

4) Disadvantages

Cons
  • Distraction and time overuse; productivity loss.
  • Misinformation and echo chambers.
  • Privacy risks and data security concerns.
  • Cyberbullying, harassment, and mental‑health impact.
  • Comparison culture leading to low self‑esteem.

5) Positive Effects of Social Media

Impact
  • Amplifies social causes and disaster relief efforts.
  • Builds professional networks and job visibility.
  • Encourages creativity, collaboration, and peer learning.
  • Enables small businesses through low‑cost promotion.
  • Offers emotional support communities.
